Asker and Svelvikstrømmen

The most available kitespots from Asker are Verket, or Trulsestranda (Strandgaten 49, Svelvik).

Trulsestranda offers shallow waters over an area of 600 × 300 m. Many kiters enjoy the additional wind created by opposing currents in Svelvikstrømmen, which can reach speeds of up to 5 knots. We advice beginners to practice waterstart with good proximity to the shipping lane.

Experienced riders also kite from Holmenskjæret (E), Sjøstrand (NE), Terneskjær in Vollen (NE-E-SE), Trettodden at Nesøya (W-SW), or Bjerkøyskjæra which must be accessed by boat.

Planning when to kite in Svelvik

The weather forecast for Svelvikstrømmen help determine when conditions are suitable for kiting. Many also use an app called Windfinder. Sunny weather and a forecasted southerly wind usually make conditions suitable for kiting between 15:00 and 19:00.

There are two wind meters near Svelvik, at Bokerøya and Verket molo. Vind nå provides a map showing wind measurements from weather stations operated by Kystverket and other sources. It is a useful tool, as it can take some time for the wind to move further into the fjord.

There are also tide and ocean currents forecasts available, and Kystverket have installed a current meter.
